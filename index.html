Problem

The company SEaNSOR envisions combating global environmental issues. Specifically, it is dedicated to reducing water pollution. Furthermore, it aims to put an end to a significant problem with impacts on the marine ecosystem and the global economy, which is illegal fishing.

Marine pollution

• Annually, 10,000,000 tons of waste are dumped into our seas.

• Plastic pieces in the ocean reach a staggering 5.25 trillion.

Illegal fishing

• Every minute, 5,000,000 fish are killed, out of which 2,000,000 are a result of illegal fishing.

• It is alarming that 1 in 5 fish caught is a product of illegal fishing.

Solution

IoT Solution

The product incorporates sound and distance sensors that detect the size, depth, and potential type of objects present on the seafloor of ports. They target areas with high accumulations of waste and pollution, and they also detect suspicious movements of fishing vessels in prohibited coastal areas or private fish farms.

1.

The benefits extend to port authorities, fish farm owners, and various NGOs and companies involved in water cleanliness preservation by "fishing for debris."

2.

The benefits and innovation brought by SEaNSOR fall into the areas of safety and protection of marine fauna and flora, as well as facilitating the cleaning operations of ports.

3.

Once the prototype is developed and replicated into more affordable solutions, the product is expected to be priced between 70 and 100 euros, depending on the requirements and its value.

Market, Target, and Opportunities

The impact of these issues is significant for all of us, but it directly affects the functioning of port authorities.


•Each port authority is responsible for the protection and control of its area, as well as ensuring the cleanliness of the waters.


• Owners of fish farms show great interest in protecting their assets, making them an important target market.


• Environmental organizations advocating for water protection will be potential customers, as SEaNSOR will serve as a helpful tool for the immediate detection of waste dumped into the waters.




Did you know that

• The global market for maritime and port security systems is projected to reach $25.5 billion by 2022.


• The global fish farming market is estimated to be valued at $285.3 billion in 2022.

Competition

• Indirect competitors of SEaNSOR include applications for detecting marine debris.


• The main competitor of SEaNSOR is the monitoring and control systems implemented by ports.


• The competitive advantage of our product, SEaNSOR, is that our solution significantly addresses both problems (illegal fishing and water pollution).




• Our solution focuses on immediate threat detection to be swiftly and easily addressed, preventing the spread of pollution and minimizing harmful impacts on the marine ecosystem.



• SEaNSOR saves time, money, and effort for all stakeholders involved.
